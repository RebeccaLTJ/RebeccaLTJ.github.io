## Overview

This project focuses on developing machine learning approaches to study the progression and heterogeneity of Parkinson's disease, with an emphasis on predicting key clinical complications.

The work investigates multiple clinically relevant outcomes, including levodopa-induced dyskinesia (LID), motor fluctuations, and cognitive impairment, such as mild-cognitive impairment (PD-MCI) and subjective cognitive decline (SCD).

A central aspect of the project is the use of multiple independent clinical cohorts: LuxPARK, PPMI, and ICEBERG-to enable robust evaluation and improve generalizability of the findings.

## Methodological Approach

### Multi-Cohort Data Integration
- Analysis of longitudinal clinical data from LuxPARK, PPMI, and ICEBERG cohorts
- Cross-cohort normalization of variables across heterogeneous datasets
- Evaluation of consistency of findings across independent populations

### Machine Learning Modeling
- Application of supervised learning methods for prediction of disease complications
- Feature selection in high-dimensional clinical datasets
- Emphasis on interpretability and clinical relevance

### Longitudinal and Time-to-Event Analysis
- Modeling of time-to-complication outcomes
- Handling censored data and varying follow-up durations
- Integration of temporal information into predictive models

### Validation Strategy
- Cross-cohort validation to assess robustness and generalizability
- Avoidance of overly optimistic performance through careful data splitting strategies

## Key Contributions

= Identification of clinical predictors associated with Parkinson's disease complications across multiple cohorts
- Demonstration of the importance of cross-cohort validation in machine learning studies
- Development of reproducible analytical pipelines for longitudinal clinical data
- Contribution to improved understanding of disease heterogeneity

## Impact

- Supports early identification of patients at higher risk of developing complications
- Provides a framework for robust machine learning analysis in heterogeneous clinical datasets
- Contributes to improving study design and patient stratification in future research
