## Overview

AXON is a machine learning-driven platform developed to support clinical decision-making through predictive analytics. The system integrates longitudinal electronic medical records (EMR) with continously updated patient data to estimate the risk of recurrence clinical outcomes.

The platform was developed for patients undergoing percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI), a common procedure for coronary artery disease, which remains a leading cause of mortality. By enabling early identification of high-risk patients, AXON supports more timely and personalized clinical interventions.

## Methodological Approach

### Real-World Data Integration
- Integration of large-scale retrospective EMR datasets
- Incorporation of prospective, real-time patient data
- Handling longitudinal and heterogeneous clinical variables

### Machine Learning Modelling
- Supervised learning models for predicting post-PCI clinical outcomes
- Training on high-dimensional real-world clinical data
- Focus on discrimination performance and robustness

### Dynamic Prediction Framework
- Continuous updating of predictions using incoming data
- Moving beyond static baseline models to time-evolving risk estimation
- Maintaining model relevance in a real-world clinical setting

## Key Contributions

- Developed machine learning pipelines for real-world clinical data analysis
- Contributed to the design and implementation of a real-time predictive system
- Collaborated with cardiologists to align modeling outputs with clinical needs
- Supported translation of analytical models into a deployable decision-support tool

## Impact
- Enabled early identification of high-risk patients following PCI
- Supported clinicians in optimizing patient management strategies
- Contributed to improved clinical decision-making in a real-world setting
- Demonstrated scalability of the framework to other disease areas

AXON received the **Malaysia Technology Excellence Awards 2022 (Medical Technology - Pharmaceuticals Category)**, recognizing its contribution to digital health innovation.

## Notes

This project highlights the application of machine learning and real-world evidence in a deployed healthcare solution, with emphasis on robustness, clinical relevance, and impact at scale.
