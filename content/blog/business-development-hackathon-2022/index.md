From 20-22 May 2022, I participated in the Business Development Hackathon *#MayYourSourceOpen*, organized by SECURITYMADEIN.LU at Technoport Belval, Luxembourg. The event brought together researchers and professionals from diverse backgrounds to develop innovative business concepts based on open-source cybersecurity tools.

I was part of the **MIMICS project team**, alongside Karthick Panner Selvam and Narjes Rahmani Mohaved. Our team was awarded **1st place**, reconizing both the technical and strategic strength of our proposed solution.

🔗 Full announcement: https://www.securitymadein.lu/cyber/news/a-successful-mayyoursourcebeopen-hackathon/

## From Data Science to Real-World Innovation

The objective of the hackathon was to transform advanced cybersecurity tools-developed under major EU projects such as SPARTA and CONCORDIA—into viable, real-world applications. This required a shift from purely technical thinking to a more holistic perspective that integrates **technology, usability, and business value**.

Our project, **MIMICS**, focused on:
> Protecting building control and automation protocols from cyber threats

We leveraged multiple open-source tools, including:
- MEMEA (CESNET)
- Vacsine (CETIC)
- DDoS Clearing House (CONCORDIA)

The challenge was not only to understand these tools, but also to combine them into a coherent solution that addresses real-world cybersecurity risks.

## Working in a Multidisciplinary Team

The hackathon environment was intense and highly collaborative. Within just 48 hours, we moved from ideation to a fully developed concept and final pitch.

Working in a cross-functional team required:
- Translating technical concepts into practical solutions
- Aligning quickly on a shared direction
- Communicating ideas clearly to both technical and non-technical audiences

This experience reinforced the importance of **interdisciplinary collaboration**, especially when working at the intersection of data, technology, and real-world applications.

## Key Learnings

This experience significantly broadened my perspective as a data scientist and researcher.

One of the most important takeaways was the ability to **translate analytical thinking into actionable impact**. In research, the focus is often on methodological rigor, but in this setting, success depend equally on how effectively we could position our solution in a real-world context.

I also developed a stronger appreciation for:
- **Problem framing** - drafting the right problem is critical
- **Communication** - conveying complex ideas in a clear and compelling way
- **Agility** - making decisions and interating rapidly under time constraints

## Outcome and Recognition

Our team was awarded **1st place** among seven competing teams. As part of this recognition, we were invited to continue developing the concept through a **12-month mentorship programme**, with additional support tied to project milestones.

Beyond the award, the experience itself was highly impactful. It demonstrated how **data-driven thinking, when combined with domain knowledge and collaboration, can lead to meaningful and scalable solutions**.

## Lasting Impact

Although my primary work focuses on biomedical data science and machine learning, this experience has had a lasting influence on how I approach research and applied problems.

It reinforced that:
- Technical solutions must align with real-world needs
- Interpretability and usability are essential for adoption
- Collaboration across domains is key to innovation

Ultimately, the hackathon highlighted that the value of data science lies not only in building models, but in **creating solutions that can be translated into real-world impact**.

---

Participating in this hackathon was both challenging and awarding. It pushed me beyond my usual domain and strengthened skills that continue to shape my work today.
